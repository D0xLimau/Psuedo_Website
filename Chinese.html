Fried Rice Recipe

Ingredients for Fried Rice:

  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 125g boneless and skinless chicken breast, cut into cubes
  • 125g shrimp, peeled, shelled and deveined
  • 1 cup frozen mixed vegetables (thawed)
  • 350g leftover steamed white rice
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1/4 teaspoon oyster sauce
  • 3 dashes ground white pepper
  • 2 eggs (lightly beaten)
  • salt to taste

Instructions:

Step 1: Heat up a wok or pan with the oil. Add the garlic and stir-fry until aromatic, follow by the chicken, shrimp, and mixed vegetables. Stir-fry until the chicken and shrimp are half cooked.

Step 2: Add in the rice and stir well with the ingredients. Add the fish sauce, soy sauce, oyster sauce, white pepper and continue to stir the fried rice for a couple of minutes.

Step 3: Using the spatula, push the rice to the side of the wok and make a "well" in the middle of the fried rice. Pour the beaten eggs in the "well." Wait for 30 seconds and then cover the eggs with the fried rice.

Step 4: Leave it for 30 seconds to 1 minute and continue to stir-fry, so the eggs form into small pieces and mix well with the fried rice. Add salt to taste and do some quick stirs, dish out and serve hot.

Crab Rangoon Recipe

Ingredients for Crab Rangoon:

  • Wonton wrappers
  • Crab Meat
  • Cream Cheese
  • Sugar
  • Salt
  • Oil

Instructions:

Step 1: Mix all the cream cheese, crab meat, sugar, and salt in a bowl. Stir to blend well. Place about 1 tablespoon of the cream cheese filling in the middle of a wonton wrapper.

Step 2: Dab some water on the outer edges of the wonton wrapper and fold the two ends of the wrapper together. Fold the other two ends to make a tiny parcel pictured below. Pinch to seal tight and make sure that there is no leakage.

Step 3: Heat up a pot of oil for deep-frying and fry the rangoons until golden brown. Dish out with a strainer or slotted spoon, draining the excess oil by laying them on a dish lined with paper towels.

Step 4: Let them cool down a bit before serving them with sweet and sour sauce.

Chinese BBQ Pork Recipe

Ingredients for Chinese BBQ Pork:

  • 500g pork belly (pork shoulder, pork butt or pork tenderloin, cut into 3 to 4 long strips)
  • 3 cloves garlic (finely chopped)
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil

Ingredients for Chinese BBQ Pork Sauce:

  • 1 1/2 tablespoons maltose
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons hoisin sauce
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Chinese rose wine or Shaoxing wine
  • 3 dashes ground white pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon five spice powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon sesame oil

Instructions:

Step 1: Add all the ingredients in the Chinese BBQ Pork Sauce in a sauce pan, heat it up on low heat. Stir to combine well. The sauce is ready when it thickens and becomes sticky. (It yields 1/2 cup sauce.) Transfer out and let cool.

Step 2: Rub the garlic on the pork. Add 2/3 of the sauce to marinate the pork for at least 4 hours, or best overnight.

Step 3: Add the cooking oil to the remaining sauce. Keep the sauce in the fridge.

Step 4: The next day, heat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a baking tray with parchment paper or aluminum foil. Place the pork on the baking tray and roast for 20 minutes. (Shake off the excess sauce before roasting.)

Step 5: Transfer the pork out of the oven. Let cool and thread the pork pieces on metal skewers. Brush the remaining char siu sauce on the pork and grill them over a fire on an outdoor grill. You may also grill them over fire on a gas cooktop.

Step 6: If you don't have either, you may select the Broil function on your oven and broil each side of the pork, about 1 minute each, or until slightly charred.

Step 7: Slice the Chinese BBQ pork into bite-size pieces, drizzle the remaining sauce over, and serve immediately with steamed white rice.

Kung Pao Chicken Recipe

Ingredients for Kung Pao Chicken:

  • Chicken breast
  • Dried Red Chilies
  • Roasted Peanuts
  • Garlic
  • Ginger
  • Scallion
  • Cooking Oil

Ingredients for Kung Pao Sauce:

  • Cornstarch
  • Shaoxing Wine
  • Oil
  • Chinese Black Vinegar
  • Dark Soy Sauce
  • Soy Sauce
  • Sugar
  • Water

Instructions:

Step 1: Rinse the chicken in water, then pat it dry with a paper towel. Cut the chicken meat into small cubes and marinate for 30 minutes.

Step 2: Mix the sauce ingredients in a small bowl and set it aside.

Step 3: Heat a wok or skillet with one tablespoon of oil and stir-fry the marinated chicken until it is 70% cooked. Dish it out and set it aside.

Step 4: Clean the wok or skillet and heat the remaining 2 tablespoons of oil until fully heated. Add the ginger and garlic slices, and quickly stir-fry before adding the dried red chilies.

Step 5: Stir-fry the dried red chilies until aromatic and fragrant, then add the chicken meat. Stir-fry briefly before adding the roasted peanuts.

Step 6: Add the sauce and stir continuously until the chicken meat is nicely coated. Then, add the scallions and stir to combine well with the chicken.

Step 7: Dish out and serve immediately with steamed rice.

Shrimp Lo Mein Recipe

Ingredients for Shrimp Lo Mein:

  • 200g dry Lo Mein noodles
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 230g shrimp
  • 175g bok choy, sliced
  • 175g red bell pepper, sliced
  • white sesame seeds

Ingredients for Shrimp Lo Mein Sauce:

  • 1 1/2 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon dark soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil

Instructions:

Step 1: Cook the lo mein according to package instructions. Rinse with cold water after cooking, drain and set aside.

Step 2: Prepare the Sauce in a small bowl. Stir to mix well. Set aside.

Step 3: Heat up a skillet on high heat. Add the cooking oil. Saute the garlic, then add the shrimp and stir-fry until the surface turns white. Add the bok choy and red bell pepper, stir to combine well.

Step 4: Add the Sauce into the skillet, stir to mix with the ingredients. Transfer the Lo Mein into the skillet, stir to coat well with the sauce and combine well with the ingredients. Top the lo mein with the white sesame, serve hot.

General Tso Chicken Recipe

Ingredients for General Tso Chicken:

  • 280g boneless skinless chicken meat, (thigh or breast, cut into bite-sized pieces)
  • 1/2 tablespoon Shaoxing wine
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 1/3 cup corn starch
  • oil for deep frying
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons oil
  • 3 slices peeled ginger (finely minced)
  • 1 clove garlic ((finely minced))
  • 4-5 dried red chilies (rinsed and seeded)
  • 2 stalks scallion (white-part only, cut into 1-inch (2.5cm) lengths)

Ingredients for General Tso Chicken Sauce:

  • 2 1/2 - 3 tablespoons Chinese rice vinegar
  • 2 1/2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1/2 tablespoon dark soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon Hoisin sauce
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 2 1/2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1/2 tablespoon Shaoxing Wine
  • 1 tablespoon corn starch

Instructions:

Step 1: Marinate the chicken meat with the Shaoxing wine and salt for 15 minutes. Mix all the ingredients for the Sauce in a small bowl. Set aside.

Step 2: Coat the chicken generously with the 1/3 cup cornstarch. Heat up the oil for deep-frying. Deep fry the chicken until they turn light brown. Dish out with a strainer, draining the excess oil by laying the chicken on a dish lined with paper towels.

Step 3: Heat up a wok with 1 1/2 tablespoons oil. Add the minced ginger, garlic, and dried chilies into the wok, stir-fry until you smell the spicy aroma of the chilies. Pour the sauce into the wok.

Step 4: When the sauce boils and thickens, add the chicken, stir to combine well with the sauce. Add the scallion and stir a few times, dish out and serve immediately.
